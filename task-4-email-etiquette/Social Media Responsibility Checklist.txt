Social Media Responsibility Checklist

Do's -
- Use a Professional Profile Picture: Use clear headshot with a neutral background.
- Give Credits where it's due: If you share a peer's project or a creator's infographic, always tag them.
- Networking over Browsing: Use platforms like LinkedIn or X to follow industry leaders to stay updated.
- Fact-Check before Sharing: Verify news or rumors though official channels before reposting to prevent the spread of misinformation.
- Curate your Profiles: Regularly see your privacy settings and delete old unprofessional posts that don't reflect who you are now.

Don'ts -
- Avoid oversharing: Never post your exact location, passwords, etc. This is a major security risk.
- Never Engage in Arguments: Avoid Arguments as it sets digital footprints and can hurt future background checks.
- Don't Post Sensitive Data: Never share screenshots of your grades, exam papers, or private IDs.
- Avoid Plagiarism: Don't claim someone else's code, design, or research as your own.
- Don't Mix Professional and Personal: Avoid using unprofessional handles (e.g. coolGamer99) for your accounts.
